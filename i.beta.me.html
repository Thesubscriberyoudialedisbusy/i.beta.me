Helium-3 (He-3, also written as 3He, see also helion) is a light,

non-radioactive isotope of helium with two protons and one neutron

(common helium having two protons and two neutrons).

Its hypothetical existence was first proposed in 1934 by the Australian

nuclear physicist Mark Oliphant while he was working at the University

of Cambridge Cavendish Laboratory.
